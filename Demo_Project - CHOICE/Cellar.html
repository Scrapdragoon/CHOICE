Cellar

The cellar fared no better. Countless bottles and mugs lay smashed upon the ground, covered in layers of dust and dirt; the sorry sight making your eyes sting with the prick of tears. (That, or maybe it was due to the sneeze you were holding back; the particles floating in the air were irritating your delicate inner nose hairs quite eagerly.) Not even the smell of yeast remained. Whatever happened here, you're far too late to do anything about it.

You ascend the staircase again to the sound of blood flowing from your hand, the steady drip your only company.
